🌊 Nice, France
April 2026 · Côte d'Azur · 20–22°C expected
Oceanice Convention Centre · Promenade des Anglais area
🌤️
Weather & Getting Around
Mid-April in Nice is genuinely pleasant — 18–22°C, mostly sunny, low chance of rain. Light jacket for evenings. The city centre is very walkable. Tram line 2 runs along the Promenade. Taxis are reliable and not expensive. Airport is 7km from centre — about €25 by taxi.
🍽️
Local food worth trying
  • Socca — chickpea pancake, Nice street food classic
  • Pan bagnat — the local take on a salade niçoise sandwich
  • Pissaladière — onion tart with anchovies & olives
  • Daube niçoise — slow-cooked beef stew, Provençal style
  • Rosé wines — Provence produces half of France's rosé
📍
Neighbourhoods
  • Vieux-Nice — old town, narrow streets, evening restaurants
  • Promenade des Anglais — 7km seafront walk
  • Le Port — quieter, good seafood restaurants
  • Cimiez — Matisse museum, Roman ruins, uphill
🥂
Evening — ITAD on the Town (Day 2)
The event's official evening is at Chez Simon and Perlone in the old town — both are well-regarded spots, good food, informal atmosphere. The old town is best explored on foot; streets are narrow and GPS gets confused. Expect noise and atmosphere.
💡
A few things about Nice
Nice only became officially French in 1860 — it was part of the Kingdom of Sardinia before that. The local dialect (Niçard) is closer to Italian than French. The city has the second-busiest airport in France after Paris. And the Promenade des Anglais was literally built by English tourists in the 19th century who wanted a place to walk.
☀️
Beach & outdoors
Nice beaches are pebbles, not sand — bring shoes you can slip off easily. The water is clear and already 17–18°C in April (cold but swimmable for the brave). Castle Hill (Colline du Château) is a 10-minute walk up for a remarkable view over the bay — worth it if you have 45 minutes free.
🗺️
Practical notes
Language: French is default but English works well in tourist areas and hospitality. A simple "Bonjour" before speaking English goes a long way.

Payment: Cards accepted everywhere, but some old-town stalls prefer cash.

Time zone: CEST — same as Netherlands (UTC+2 in April).

Emergency: 15 (SAMU medical), 17 (police), 18 (fire), 112 (EU general).
